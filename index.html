Radically Distributed Ownership

Wireless community owned networks (WCNs) are fundamentally different than commercial or municipal ISPs. Rather than public ownership of a central infrastructure like in a municipal network, ownership of our network is distributed. In a WCN each router is an active participant in the transmission of data. Such networks, because of their interwoven nature, are called “mesh” networks, and they allow communities to move beyond producer/consumer relationships with their ISP towards a more participatory or neighborly model within their WCN.

In addition to providing connectivity, mesh networks enable new forms of social structures, foster social inclusion, increase community interaction, increase the number of physical meetings of neighbors, and boost community cohesiveness. Most of the time, owners of nodes that are connected to each other know each other personally.

Secure And Private Communications

Our network is constructed with a high level of security and privacy built in. All traffic is encrypted and the identity of participants is obscured from the world outside our network.
